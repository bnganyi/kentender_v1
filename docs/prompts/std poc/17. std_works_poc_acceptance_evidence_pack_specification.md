## 2. Purpose

The purpose of this step is to define the formal acceptance checklist and evidence pack for STD-WORKS-POC.

Step 16 proves that the POC can run end-to-end. Step 17 packages that proof into a structured acceptance artefact suitable for product review, legal/compliance review, procurement SME review, and technical handoff.

The acceptance pack shall answer four questions:

1. What exactly did the POC prove?
2. What evidence supports that conclusion?
3. What limitations are explicitly acknowledged?
4. What decisions are required before moving toward production design or implementation?

---

## 3. Design Principle

The acceptance evidence pack shall implement the principle:

> A legally foundational procurement-system POC is not accepted merely because it runs; it is accepted because its claims, evidence, boundaries, and risks are formally documented.

The evidence pack shall avoid vague success claims. Every acceptance claim shall be tied to a specific observable result, screenshot, log, generated record, package file, or smoke-test result.

The pack shall be formal enough to prevent later development drift, but concise enough to be reviewed by decision-makers.

---

## 4. Scope of Step 17

Step 17 includes:

1. Creating the POC acceptance checklist.
2. Creating the evidence index.
3. Recording package identity and hashes.
4. Recording smoke-test results.
5. Recording expected and observed required forms.
6. Recording expected and observed BoQ behavior.
7. Recording validation and negative scenario behavior.
8. Recording preview output verification.
9. Recording audit output verification.
10. Recording known limitations.
11. Recording accepted issues and defects.
12. Capturing reviewer sign-off fields.
13. Preparing a final acceptance decision recommendation.

---

## 5. Non-Scope of Step 17

The following are explicitly out of scope:

1. Fixing implementation defects unless they block evidence capture.
2. Adding new POC features.
3. Production gap analysis. That belongs to Step 18.
4. Full legal review of the complete PPRA STD text.
5. PDF certification.
6. Bidder portal implementation.
7. Bid submission workflow.
8. Evaluation workflow.
9. Award or contract execution workflow.
10. Security audit or penetration testing.
11. Full automated test suite.

If Step 17 reveals gaps, they shall be recorded and dispositioned. They shall not be silently folded into scope.

## 8. Acceptance Summary Requirements

The acceptance summary shall state the overall outcome using one of these values:

| Outcome | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `Accepted` | POC met acceptance criteria; no blocker issues remain |
| `Accepted with Limitations` | POC met core acceptance criteria; non-blocking limitations or deferred issues remain |
| `Conditionally Accepted` | POC may proceed only after listed conditions are resolved |
| `Rejected` | POC failed core acceptance criteria |

Recommended POC decision standard:

The STD-WORKS-POC should be accepted only if Step 16 primary flow passes and required negative scenarios block preview generation correctly.
