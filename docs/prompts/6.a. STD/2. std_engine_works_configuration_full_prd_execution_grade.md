## 1. Purpose of This Full PRD

This Full PRD defines the product behavior of the **STD Engine Works Configuration** capability.

It specifies how the system converts legally prescribed PPRA Works Standard Tender Documents into structured, reusable, versioned, enforceable system configurations that downstream procurement modules can consume without interpretation.

This PRD exists because the platform cannot safely implement Tender Management v2 until the STD Engine produces real structured outputs. Tender Management v2 depends on the STD Engine for the following legally material outputs:

1. the supplier-facing generated tender document bundle;
2. the derived supplier submission model;
3. the derived bid opening model;
4. the derived evaluation model;
5. the derived contract carry-forward model;
6. addendum impact analysis and regenerated outputs;
7. readiness validation evidence.

This PRD defines:

1. the product problem;
2. the legal and operational basis;
3. the reusable STD Engine architecture pattern;
4. the Works-specific configuration behavior;
5. template/version/profile lifecycle behavior;
6. Works STD instance configuration behavior;
7. Works Requirements behavior;
8. BOQ behavior;
9. forms and supplier submission derivation;
10. opening model derivation;
11. evaluation model derivation;
12. contract model derivation;
13. addendum impact behavior;
14. readiness validation behavior;
15. cross-module contracts;
16. audit and immutability requirements;
17. acceptance criteria and completion gates.

This PRD does not define the final database schema. The Strict Domain Model defines entities, fields, constraints, and relationships. This PRD defines what the product must do, how users and system processes interact with it, and what behavior must be enforced.

## 2. Product Problem

The e-Procurement platform previously treated Standard Tender Documents as files, attachments, or uploaded document bundles. That approach is not adequate for a legally controlled public procurement system.

A file-based STD approach creates the following unacceptable risks:

1. Tender Management may manually define supplier submission requirements that do not match the published STD;
2. Bid Opening may use an opening register not derived from the STD;
3. Evaluation may use criteria not disclosed in the STD;
4. Contract Management may draft contract terms that diverge from tender terms;
5. addenda may change one artifact without regenerating affected downstream rules;
6. auditors may be unable to reconstruct how tender content produced submission, opening, evaluation, and contract behavior;
7. suppliers may be exposed to inconsistent or hidden rules;
8. PPRA standard sections that must remain unmodified may be altered accidentally or silently;
9. Works BOQ quantities may be incorrectly treated as supplier-editable;
10. arithmetic correction may be performed at the wrong lifecycle stage.

The product problem is therefore:

> The system must transform PPRA Works Standard Tender Documents into structured, governed, machine-readable templates and tender-specific STD instances so that all downstream procurement behavior is derived from the same legally controlled source of truth.

## 3. Product Goal

The product goal is to build an STD Engine Works Configuration capability that allows the platform to configure, approve, activate, instantiate, validate, generate, amend, and audit PPRA-aligned Works STDs in a structured and legally defensible manner.

The system must ensure that:

1. Works STDs are represented as structured templates, not uncontrolled uploaded documents;
2. PPRA Works sections are preserved as the controlling legal structure;
3. Instructions to Tenderers and General Conditions of Contract are locked standard text;
4. Tender Data Sheet and Special Conditions of Contract are completed through structured parameters;
5. Evaluation and Qualification Criteria are structured and cannot be extended downstream;
6. Tendering Forms generate supplier submission requirements;
7. Works Requirements are structured and may include section-bound controlled attachments;
8. BOQ is modeled as first-class structured data;
9. supplier BOQ inputs are constrained according to Works rules;
10. derived submission, opening, evaluation, and contract models are generated deterministically;
11. addenda trigger formal impact analysis and regeneration where required;
12. all outputs are versioned, immutable when published or used, and auditable.

## 4. Legal and Operational Basis

The Works Configuration must reflect the structure and controls present in the PPRA Works Standard Tender Documents.

The system must recognize that the Works STD is not a single flat document. It is a structured legal instrument composed of Parts and Sections.

The Works STD includes:

1. Part 1 — Tendering Procedures;
2. Section I — Instructions to Tenderers;
3. Section II — Tender Data Sheet;
4. Section III — Evaluation and Qualification Criteria;
5. Section IV — Tendering Forms;
6. Part 2 — Procuring Entity / Works Requirements;
7. Bills of Quantities or Activity Schedule, depending on Works profile;
8. Specifications;
9. Drawings;
10. Part 3 — Conditions of Contract and Contract Forms;
11. Section VIII — General Conditions of Contract;
12. Section IX — Special Conditions of Contract;
13. Section X — Contract Forms.

The system must also enforce the legal meaning of those sections:

| STD Area | Legal / Operational Meaning | Required System Control |
|---|---|---|
| Instructions to Tenderers | Standard tendering procedure text. | Locked; no tender-instance text editing. |
| Tender Data Sheet | Tender-specific supplement to ITT. | Parameter-only completion; no uncontrolled additions. |
| Evaluation and Qualification Criteria | Exclusive disclosed evaluation criteria. | Structured criteria; no downstream manual injection. |
| Tendering Forms | Forms and schedules tenderers must submit. | Drives derived submission model. |
| Works Requirements | Technical and execution requirements. | Structured components plus section-bound attachments. |
| BOQ / Activity Schedule | Pricing and contract-value structure. | First-class structured pricing model. |
| General Conditions of Contract | Standard contract clauses. | Locked; no tender-instance text editing. |
| Special Conditions of Contract | Tender-specific supplement to GCC. | Parameter-only completion. |
| Contract Forms | Forms forming part of contract after award. | Drives contract carry-forward model. |

The system must also recognize the evaluation discipline in the Works STD: evaluation must use the criteria and methodologies disclosed in Section III. The Procuring Entity may complete permitted parts of the criteria as guided by the STD, but downstream modules must not introduce undisclosed criteria.

## 5. Core Product Principle

The STD Engine Works Configuration must enforce this principle:

> The Works STD is a structured legal source-of-truth. Every downstream tender behavior must be generated from it or explicitly traced to it.

This means:

1. Tender Management consumes the generated bundle and derived model references;
2. Supplier Portal consumes supplier-facing bundle, attachments, addenda, and DSM;
3. Bid Submission validates bids against DSM;
4. Bid Opening uses DOM;
5. Evaluation uses DEM;
6. Contract Management uses DCM;
7. Audit reconstructs the full chain from template version to tender instance to generated outputs to downstream handoffs.

No module may use file uploads, manual configuration, or hidden rules as an alternative source of truth.

## 10. Template Family Requirements

### 10.1 Purpose

The system must maintain a canonical Works STD template family. The template family is not a single uploaded document. It is a controlled container for template versions, applicability profiles, structured sections, and generation rules.

### 10.2 Required Behavior

The system must allow authorized STD Administrators to:

1. create a Works template family in Draft state;
2. assign issuing authority metadata;
3. classify it as Works;
4. define supported procurement methods;
5. submit it for review;
6. approve it;
7. activate it;
8. suspend it;
9. archive it only where no legal dependency is violated.

### 10.3 Prohibited Behavior

The system must not allow:

1. multiple uncontrolled Works template families for each minor Works variant;
2. creating BOQ, drawings, or specifications as independent top-level STD families;
3. deleting a template family that has active versions or used tender instances;
4. using an inactive family for new tender instances.

### 10.4 Acceptance Criteria

| ID | Acceptance Criterion |
|---|---|
| STD-PRD-TF-AC-001 | `STD-WORKS` can be created as the canonical Works template family. |
| STD-PRD-TF-AC-002 | Works variants are handled by applicability profiles, not uncontrolled template families. |
| STD-PRD-TF-AC-003 | A template family used by any active template version cannot be deleted. |
| STD-PRD-TF-AC-004 | A non-active family cannot be used to create a new template version for tender use. |

## 11. Template Version Requirements

### 11.1 Purpose

A template version represents a legally controlled release of the Works STD structure. It is the parent of all sections, clauses, parameters, forms, BOQ rules, mappings, readiness rules, and generation behavior for that version.

### 11.2 Version Lifecycle

The system must support the following template version lifecycle:

1. Draft;
2. Structure In Progress;
3. Structure Validation Blocked;
4. Structure Validation Passed;
5. Legal / Policy Review Pending;
6. Approved;
7. Active;
8. Suspended;
9. Superseded;
10. Retired;
11. Archived.

### 11.3 Version Creation Flow

When an authorized user creates a Works template version, the system must:

1. require parent template family;
2. require official source document metadata;
3. require version label and effective date;
4. initialize Part and Section skeletons according to Works STD structure;
5. require configuration of section editability;
6. require definition of parameter schema;
7. require forms and extraction mappings before activation;
8. prevent activation until structure validation passes;
9. record audit event.

### 11.4 Activation Preconditions

A template version may become Active only if:

1. the parent family is Active;
2. all mandatory Parts are present;
3. all mandatory Sections are present;
4. ITT section is locked;
5. GCC section is locked;
6. TDS and SCC are parameter-only;
7. Section III has structured evaluation rule templates or mappings;
8. Section IV has form definitions;
9. Works Requirements components are defined;
10. BOQ or applicable pricing schedule is defined for profiles requiring it;
11. extraction mappings exist for DSM, DOM, DEM, DCM;
12. readiness rules exist;
13. legal/policy review is approved;
14. structure validation status is Pass.

### 11.5 Immutability

Once Active, a template version is immutable. Any change to active legal structure requires a new template version.

### 11.6 Acceptance Criteria

| ID | Acceptance Criterion |
|---|---|
| STD-PRD-TV-AC-001 | Active template version cannot be edited directly. |
| STD-PRD-TV-AC-002 | Used template version cannot be deleted. |
| STD-PRD-TV-AC-003 | Activation is blocked if ITT or GCC is not locked. |
| STD-PRD-TV-AC-004 | Activation is blocked if Section III has no structured evaluation mappings. |
| STD-PRD-TV-AC-005 | Activation is blocked if derived model extraction mappings are incomplete. |

## 12. Applicability Profile Requirements

### 12.1 Purpose

Applicability profiles allow the same canonical Works STD family to support Works variants without fragmenting the source-of-truth model.

Profiles may distinguish:

1. building and associated civil engineering works;
2. roads / water / bridges;
3. small works;
4. maintenance works;
5. electrical/mechanical works;
6. design-build where later permitted;
7. admeasurement, lump sum, or activity schedule pricing where allowed.

### 12.2 Required Profile Behavior

Each profile must define:

1. applicable Works type;
2. allowed procurement methods;
3. contract type;
4. pricing model;
5. whether BOQ is mandatory;
6. whether drawings are mandatory;
7. whether specifications are mandatory;
8. whether site information is mandatory;
9. whether environmental/social requirements are mandatory;
10. whether HSE requirements are mandatory;
11. whether lots are supported;
12. whether alternative tenders are supported;
13. whether margin of preference may be configured;
14. whether reservations may be configured;
15. readiness rules applicable to that profile.

### 12.3 Profile Selection Flow

When creating a tender-specific STD instance, the system must:

1. receive procurement category and method context from Tender Management;
2. show only active compatible Works profiles;
3. reject incompatible profile selection;
4. initialize only applicable sections, requirements, forms, and readiness rules;
5. record the selected profile permanently on the STD instance.

### 12.4 Acceptance Criteria

| ID | Acceptance Criterion |
|---|---|
| STD-PRD-PROFILE-AC-001 | Building/Civil profile can require BOQ, specifications, drawings, site information, HSE, and environmental/social requirements. |
| STD-PRD-PROFILE-AC-002 | Small Works profile can use a different section ordering where the source STD requires it while still using the same core engine. |
| STD-PRD-PROFILE-AC-003 | Incompatible profile cannot be selected for a non-Works tender. |
| STD-PRD-PROFILE-AC-004 | Profile-specific omissions require reason and audit where legal content is affected. |

## 13. Section and Clause Configuration Requirements

### 13.1 Section Requirements

The system must model Works sections as structured records with defined editability.

| Section | Required Editability | Required Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Invitation to Tender | Parameterized / generated | Completed from structured tender parameters. |
| Section I — ITT | Locked | Used without modification. |
| Section II — TDS | Parameter only | Completed by Procuring Entity through controlled parameters only. |
| Section III — Evaluation and Qualification Criteria | Structured | Configured only using permitted criteria templates and mappings. |
| Section IV — Tendering Forms | Structured / generated | Drives supplier submission requirements. |
| Works Requirements | Structured | Contains scope, specifications, drawings, site, HSE, environmental/social requirements. |
| BOQ / Activity Schedule | Structured | Defines Works pricing and contract-value structure. |
| GCC | Locked | Used without modification. |
| SCC | Parameter only | Supplements GCC using controlled parameters. |
| Contract Forms | Generated / structured | Drives contract carry-forward. |

### 13.2 Clause Requirements

The system must classify clauses as:

1. locked standard clauses;
2. parameterized clauses;
3. structured editable clauses;
4. generated clauses;
5. optional profile-controlled clauses;
6. clauses that drive DSM;
7. clauses that drive DOM;
8. clauses that drive DEM;
9. clauses that drive DCM.

### 13.3 Clause Editing Rules

1. Clauses in ITT must not be text-edited in a tender instance.
2. Clauses in GCC must not be text-edited in a tender instance.
3. TDS and SCC clauses must be completed only through parameter values.
4. Section III clauses must be represented as structured evaluation rule templates and mappings.
5. Optional clauses may be omitted only where profile permits and omission reason is recorded.
6. Any clause that drives a derived model must have an extraction mapping.

### 13.4 Acceptance Criteria

| ID | Acceptance Criterion |
|---|---|
| STD-PRD-SEC-AC-001 | User cannot edit ITT or GCC text in an STD instance. |
| STD-PRD-SEC-AC-002 | User completes TDS and SCC through parameter inputs only. |
| STD-PRD-SEC-AC-003 | Section III criteria are structured and source-traceable. |
| STD-PRD-SEC-AC-004 | A clause marked as DSM/DOM/DEM/DCM-driving cannot be activated without extraction mapping. |

| Contract | Captures completion period, performance security, retention, defects liability period, insurance, payment terms, project manager details. |
| Complaints | Captures complaint recipient, position, address, email, complaint procedure reference. |

### 14.3 Parameter Dependency Requirements

The system must support conditional dependencies.

Examples:

| Trigger | Required Result |
|---|---|
| Site visit required = Yes | Site visit date, location, contact become required. |
| Pre-tender meeting required = Yes | Meeting date, time, venue/link become required. |
| Tender security required = Yes | Security amount, form, currency, validity become required. |
| Margin of preference applies = Yes | DEM must include margin methodology. |
| Lots enabled = Yes | BOQ, submission, opening, evaluation, and contract models must support lots. |
| Alternative tenders permitted = Yes | DSM and DEM must include alternative-tender rules. |
| Subcontracting permitted = Yes | DSM must require subcontracting disclosure where threshold applies. |
| Reservations apply = Yes | Eligibility and evaluation rules must include reservation criteria. |

### 14.4 Parameter Validation Requirements

The system must validate:

1. required values are present;
2. values have correct data type;
3. date/time values are coherent;
4. monetary amounts have currency;
5. percentages are within valid bounds;
6. conditional dependencies are satisfied;
7. parameter values that drive generated models have mappings;
8. post-publication parameter changes go through addendum.

### 14.5 Acceptance Criteria

| ID | Acceptance Criterion |
|---|---|
| STD-PRD-PARAM-AC-001 | Missing required TDS parameter blocks readiness. |
| STD-PRD-PARAM-AC-002 | Missing required SCC parameter blocks DCM generation/readiness. |
| STD-PRD-PARAM-AC-003 | Enabling tender security makes security amount and acceptable form required. |
| STD-PRD-PARAM-AC-004 | Enabling margin of preference causes DEM to include configured preference logic. |
| STD-PRD-PARAM-AC-005 | Changing a published parameter requires addendum impact analysis. |

## 15. Works Requirements Configuration Requirements

### 15.1 Purpose

Works Requirements describe the technical, site, execution, environmental, social, health, and safety obligations of the Works contract.

They must be structured. Attachments may support the requirements, but they must not replace the structured Works Requirements model where structured data is required.

### 15.2 Required Works Requirement Components

The system must support the following components.

| Component | Required Behavior |
|---|---|
| Scope of Works | Structured description of works to be executed. |
| Technical Specifications | Structured specifications and section-bound specification attachments. |
| Drawing Register | List of drawings with version, file reference, discipline, supplier-facing flag. |
| Site Information | Site location, access, constraints, possession, known risks. |
| Environmental and Social Requirements | Environmental, social, and compliance obligations. |
| Health and Safety Requirements | HSE obligations and required supplier HSE submissions. |
| Work Programme Requirement | Requirements for supplier work programme submission. |
| Method Statement Requirement | Requirements for supplier method statement submission. |
| Personnel Requirements | Key personnel categories and qualifications where required. |
| Equipment Requirements | Major equipment schedule where required. |
| Subcontracting Requirements | Permitted subcontracting and disclosure requirements. |

### 15.3 Component Completion Behavior

For each required component, the system must:

1. show completion status;
2. require structured content or explicit permitted omission;
3. require section-bound attachments where profile requires attachments;
4. mark whether the component drives DSM, DEM, DCM, or bundle only;
5. block readiness if required component is incomplete;
6. lock component after publication;
7. require addendum for post-publication changes.

### 15.4 Attachment Behavior

Section-bound attachments must:

1. belong to a specific STD instance;
2. be bound to a section and optionally a Works Requirement component;
3. have version number;
4. have file hash or controlled storage reference;
5. be classified as supplier-facing, internal-only, or contract-facing;
6. be included or excluded from generated bundle according to classification;
7. be immutable once published;
8. be superseded by new version through addendum where material.

### 15.5 Acceptance Criteria

| ID | Acceptance Criterion |
|---|---|
| STD-PRD-WR-AC-001 | Readiness is blocked if required Works Requirements component is incomplete. |
| STD-PRD-WR-AC-002 | Drawings and specifications cannot exist as unbound generic attachments. |
| STD-PRD-WR-AC-003 | Supplier-facing generated bundle includes supplier-facing Works Requirement attachments. |
| STD-PRD-WR-AC-004 | Changing a published technical specification requires addendum impact analysis. |

## 16. BOQ Configuration Requirements

### 16.1 Purpose

The BOQ is a first-class structured pricing and contract-value object for Works procurement. It must not be treated as a passive spreadsheet attachment.

The BOQ must support:

1. bills;
2. bill types;
3. item rows;
4. item descriptions;
5. units;
6. quantities;
7. provisional sums;
8. dayworks where applicable;
9. supplier rate input rules;
10. computed line amounts;
11. total computation;
12. addendum revisions;
13. evaluation-stage arithmetic correction;
14. contract carry-forward.

### 16.2 BOQ Ownership Rules

| BOQ Element | Owner | Supplier Editable? | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Bill structure | Procuring Entity | No | May change pre-publication; post-publication by addendum only. |
| Item number | Procuring Entity | No | Locked for supplier. |
| Item description | Procuring Entity | No | Locked for supplier. |
| Unit | Procuring Entity | No | Locked for supplier. |
| Quantity | Procuring Entity | No | Core Works rule. |
| Fixed provisional sum | Procuring Entity | No | Included as fixed amount unless configured otherwise. |
| Daywork schedule structure | Procuring Entity | No | Supplier pricing controlled by model. |
| Unit rate | Supplier | Yes, where rate required | Supplier rate input. |
| Computed amount | System | No | Quantity × rate or configured rule. |
| Grand total | System | No | Computed. |

### 16.3 BOQ Configuration Flow

The system must support the following flow before publication:

1. Technical Officer / Quantity Surveyor opens BOQ configuration surface;
2. user creates or imports controlled BOQ structure;
3. user defines bills;
4. user defines items under bills;
5. user enters item descriptions, units, and quantities;
6. user marks item type: measured, preliminary, provisional sum, daywork, note;
7. system applies allowed supplier input mode;
8. system validates quantities and required fields;
9. system validates fixed sums and dayworks rules;
10. system produces BOQ completeness status;
11. system exposes BOQ to DSM, DEM, and DCM generation.

### 16.4 BOQ Import Requirements

If BOQ import is supported, import must be a structured import into BOQ entities, not upload-as-source.

The system must:

1. parse bill and item rows into structured records;
2. require user review and confirmation;
3. reject ambiguous rows;
4. reject missing item descriptions, units, or quantities where required;
5. preserve original imported file only as supporting evidence;
6. not treat the uploaded file as the authoritative BOQ after structured import.

### 16.5 Supplier Pricing Rules

The generated DSM and Supplier Portal must enforce that suppliers:

1. enter rates only for rate-required measured items;
2. cannot change quantities;
3. cannot change units;
4. cannot change descriptions;
5. cannot change bill structure;
6. cannot change fixed provisional sums;
7. cannot edit computed amounts;
8. cannot submit missing required rates;
9. cannot submit negative rates;
10. cannot submit zero rates unless explicitly permitted by model.

### 16.6 Arithmetic Correction Stage

The STD Engine must generate models that enforce:

1. no arithmetic correction at submission;
2. no arithmetic correction at opening;
3. arithmetic correction only in Evaluation;
4. correction rules contained in DEM;
5. contract price source contained in DCM.

### 16.7 Acceptance Criteria

| ID | Acceptance Criterion |
|---|---|
| STD-PRD-BOQ-AC-001 | BOQ cannot pass readiness without at least one valid bill and item where profile requires BOQ. |
| STD-PRD-BOQ-AC-002 | Supplier quantity edit is impossible under generated DSM/BOQ model. |
| STD-PRD-BOQ-AC-003 | BOQ upload cannot become source-of-truth unless parsed into structured BOQ records. |
| STD-PRD-BOQ-AC-004 | BOQ change after publication requires addendum and new BOQ version. |
| STD-PRD-BOQ-AC-005 | DEM contains arithmetic correction rules; DOM does not. |
| STD-PRD-BOQ-AC-006 | DCM states corrected evaluated BOQ total as contract price source. |

1. tender reference from Tender Management;

### 24.5 Instance Locking

## 35. Non-Functional Requirements

### 35.1 Legal Defensibility

The system must preserve evidence proving that published tender outputs and downstream models were generated from the governing STD instance.

### 35.2 Determinism

The same STD instance state must generate the same outputs, except where timestamps, generated codes, or controlled version increments are expected.

### 35.3 Traceability

Every generated output must trace to:

1. template family;
2. template version;
3. applicability profile;
4. STD instance;
5. parameter values;
6. Works Requirements;
7. BOQ version;
8. attachments;
9. addendum if applicable.

### 35.4 Immutability

Active template definitions, published instances, generated outputs, readiness runs, and audit events must not be silently modified.

### 35.5 Availability

Readiness validation and generation services must be reliable enough not to block lawful tender publication workflows unnecessarily. If generation fails, it must fail explicitly with auditable error state.

### 35.6 Security

Only authorized roles may alter template structures, parameter values, BOQ content, Works Requirements, or generated outputs before lock. After lock, no ordinary role may directly mutate legal content.

### 35.7 Maintainability

Method-specific behavior must be implemented through profiles, mappings, rules, and plug-ins, not hard-coded directly into Tender Management.
