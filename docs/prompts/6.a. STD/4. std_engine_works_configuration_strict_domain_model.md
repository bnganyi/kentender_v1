## 1. Domain Model Purpose

This document defines the strict, normalized domain model for the STD Engine Works Configuration layer.

It is intended to support:

1. structured PPRA Works STD configuration;
2. reusable method-agnostic STD Engine architecture;
3. Works-specific BOQ, Works Requirements, forms, and evaluation configuration;
4. deterministic generation of derived models consumed by downstream modules;
5. legal immutability of active and used STD versions;
6. addendum impact analysis and regeneration;
7. full audit reconstruction.

This specification must be treated as authoritative for implementation.

No implementation may replace this domain model with an upload-only, attachment-first, or manually configured downstream rule model.

---

## 2. Architectural Separation

The domain model is divided into two layers.

### 2.1 Core STD Engine Layer

The Core STD Engine is reusable across procurement methods.

It owns:

1. template families;
2. template versions;
3. applicability profiles;
4. section definitions;
5. clause definitions;
6. parameter definitions;
7. form definitions;
8. generated bundles;
9. derived submission models;
10. derived opening models;
11. derived evaluation models;
12. derived contract carry-forward models;
13. readiness validations;
14. addendum impact analysis;
15. STD audit trail.

The core layer must not contain Works-only concepts such as BOQ rate-only pricing rules as global hard-coded behavior. Those belong in the Works plug-in layer.

### 2.2 Works Configuration Layer

The Works Configuration Layer extends the Core STD Engine for Works procurement.

It owns:

1. Works applicability profiles;
2. Works Requirements components;
3. structured BOQ model;
4. Works tendering forms configuration;
5. Works qualification criteria configuration;
6. Works-specific evaluation rule templates;
7. Works contract carry-forward mappings;
8. Works addendum impact rules;
9. Works readiness rules.

---

## 3. Source-Driven Structural Requirements

The model must support the PPRA Works STD structure:

1. Part 1 — Tendering Procedures;
2. Section I — Instructions to Tenderers;
3. Section II — Tender Data Sheet;
4. Section III — Evaluation and Qualification Criteria;
5. Section IV — Tendering Forms;
6. Part 2 — Works Requirements;
7. Section V — Bills of Quantities or relevant Works schedule;
8. Section VI — Specifications;
9. Section VII — Drawings;
10. Part 3 — Conditions of Contract and Contract Forms;
11. Section VIII — General Conditions of Contract;
12. Section IX — Special Conditions of Contract;
13. Section X — Contract Forms.

The model must enforce:

1. Instructions to Tenderers are locked standard text;
2. General Conditions of Contract are locked standard text;
3. Tender Data Sheet is parameterized and must not add uncontrolled new items;
4. Special Conditions of Contract supplement the GCC through structured parameters;
5. Evaluation and Qualification Criteria are structured and cannot be manually extended by downstream modules;
6. Tendering Forms drive supplier submission requirements;
7. BOQ is structured data, not a passive spreadsheet attachment;
8. Specifications and Drawings are part of Works Requirements and may have section-bound attachments;
9. Contract Forms are generated/carry-forward objects, not manually re-authored tender terms.
