Requisitions module scope summary

**Why Requisitions is next**

Kenya’s current e-GP service lineup explicitly separates **Annual Procurement Plan**, **Requisition**, **Tender Preparation**, and **Tender Opening**, which supports keeping requisitions as its own module rather than absorbing it into planning or tendering. ([EGP Kenya](https://egpkenya.go.ke/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

Your own internal PRD already positions **Purchase Requisition** as the formal start of controlled procurement demand, sitting after Strategy and Budget and before Procurement Planning. It also makes requisitions the first point for strategic enforcement and budget commitment.

OECD guidance treats the public procurement cycle as beginning with **needs assessment**, and OCDS treats planning as a distinct stage before tender, award, contract, and implementation. That supports a clean module boundary where Requisitions handles demand capture and approval, and Procurement Planning handles consolidation and scheduling. ([OECD Legal Instruments](https://legalinstruments.oecd.org/public/doc/131/131.en.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Requisitions Module — Scope Summary**

**Module purpose**

The Requisitions module formally captures, validates, approves, and hands off procurement demand.

It answers:

-   what is needed
-   by which department
-   for what strategic purpose
-   against which budget
-   with what urgency
-   with what approval outcome

This matches your internal PRD, which requires every procurement to originate from a legitimate approved need, aligned to strategy and budget, with budget commitment beginning at this stage.

**What the module is**

Requisitions is the **single intake gate** for demand.

All demand should resolve into a requisition record, whether it is:

-   manually created by a departmental user
-   generated from a plan later
-   triggered by an exception or emergency path

That is the only clean way to preserve auditability and budget/strategy linkage.

**What the module is not**

It is not:

-   Procurement Planning
-   Tender Preparation
-   Supplier Management
-   Contracting
-   Inventory/Assets

Those remain separate modules.

**Core business role in the overall system**

The lifecycle should now read:

Strategy → Budget → Requisition → Procurement Planning → Supplier Management → Tendering → Submission & Opening → Evaluation & Award → Contract Management

That keeps the boundaries clean:

-   Strategy = intent
-   Budget = allocation
-   Requisition = approved demand
-   Planning = consolidation and scheduling

**Core user flows**

**1\. Create requisition**

Actor: Department user / requisitioner

Creates a requisition with:

-   basic request details
-   line items
-   business justification
-   strategic linkage
-   budget linkage
-   delivery need

**2\. Approve requisition**

Actors:

-   HoD / configured approver
-   Finance reviewer

Approvers review, approve, return, or reject. Finance also performs budget validation/reservation. Your internal PRD already requires at least one approval step and hard budget validation at submission/approval.

**3\. Hand off to planning**

Actor: Procurement officer

Approved requisitions are marked planning-ready or accepted into Procurement Planning, preserving strategic and budget linkage. Your internal PRD explicitly says approved requisitions become plan items and must retain linkage.

**Core design principles**

**1\. Mandatory entry point**

No procurement should begin without a requisition, except controlled emergency handling. Your internal PRD states this explicitly.

**2\. Strategy enforcement point**

A requisition must be linked to the approved strategic hierarchy. Your current implementation pack uses **Performance Target** as the primary selectable field and derives the upstream hierarchy from it.

**3\. Budget control point**

Budget validation must occur here, and reservation begins here. Your internal PRD defines hard enforcement: block submission if available budget is insufficient, and create a reservation on approval.

**4\. Structured demand, not vague requests**

The requisition must use structured line items and controlled fields, not just narrative description.

**5\. Workflow-first UX**

The module should support:

-   requisitioner draft entry
-   HoD review
-   finance validation
-   procurement acceptance into planning

Your implementation pack already frames the UX this way and recommends separate work queues by role.

**Minimal domain scope**

**Core entities**

**Purchase Requisition**

Header record for the demand request.

**Purchase Requisition Item**

Child table for requested goods/services/works.

**Budget reservation / linkage artifact**

May be a ledger entry or linked reservation record, but this is a downstream effect of approval, not a separate user-facing module at this stage. Your golden scenario already models a reservation entry after approved requisition.

**Minimum required fields on requisition**

From your internal PRD and implementation pack, the module must minimally cover:

-   requisition ID
-   entity
-   department
-   requesting user
-   request date
-   required by date
-   priority
-   requisition type
-   business justification
-   technical/specification summary
-   strategic linkage
-   budget linkage
-   requested amount
-   workflow state
-   planning handoff state

**Governance model**

This must be included from day one. We are not repeating the Budget omission.

**Minimum state model**

Recommended MVP state model:

Draft → Submitted → Pending HoD Approval → Pending Finance Approval → Approved → Planning Ready

↘ Rejected / Returned ↺

This aligns with your implementation pack’s target model and work queues for requisitioner, HoD, finance, and procurement.

**Required governance behaviors**

-   Draft = editable
-   Submitted / in approval = restricted
-   Approved = locked
-   Rejected / Returned = editable again with reason
-   Finance approval triggers budget reservation
-   Cancellation releases reservation
-   Planning Ready marks downstream handoff eligibility

**Must-have actions**

-   Submit
-   Approve
-   Return
-   Reject
-   Approve & Reserve Budget
-   Mark Planning Ready

Your implementation pack already identifies these as the minimal role-relevant actions.

**Unplanned and emergency demand**

This module is the right place to handle exceptions.

Unplanned and emergency demand should not bypass requisitions. Instead, the requisition should carry a demand classification such as:

-   Planned
-   Unplanned
-   Emergency

and require:

-   justification
-   approval escalation as needed
-   audit flag
-   downstream plan amendment or exception handling

That keeps all demand auditable while preserving the rule that procurement begins from a controlled record.

**Workspace UX direction**

We already know the lesson: this module must be **dashboard first**, not form first.

**Landing page should provide**

-   KPI strip
-   work tabs
-   master-detail layout
-   role-based work queues

**Work tabs should include**

-   All
-   My Work
-   Draft
-   Pending HoD Approval
-   Pending Finance Approval
-   Approved
-   Planning Ready
-   Rejected / Returned

This matches the queues recommended in your requisition implementation pack.

**Default by role**

-   Requisitioner → My Work / Draft
-   HoD → Pending HoD Approval
-   Finance → Pending Finance Approval
-   Procurement → Planning Ready

That is how approvers find work without scanning every record.

**Builder / form direction**

The requisition entry/edit surface should be **workflow-first** and sectioned, not a giant form. Your implementation pack already defines the right section order:

-   Basic Request
-   Approval Routing
-   Items
-   Business Justification
-   Strategy Linkage
-   Budget Linkage
-   Delivery & Fulfilment
-   Flags & Exceptions
-   Planning Handoff
-   Closure
-   Sensitivity

We should treat that as the starting point, not reinvent it.

**Roles in scope**

Minimum roles now:

-   Requisitioner / Department User
-   HoD / Configured Approver
-   Finance Reviewer
-   Procurement Officer
-   Administrator

These are already reflected in your internal PRD and testing scenario.

**Seed data implications**

The requisition seed must include at least:

-   1 Draft requisition
-   1 Submitted requisition
-   1 Approved requisition with reservation
-   1 Rejected / Returned requisition
-   1 Planning Ready requisition

Your golden scenario already includes an approved requisition for ultrasound machines and its linked budget reservation and plan item, which is a good anchor scenario.

**Smoke contract implications**

The requisition smoke suite must minimally prove:

-   requisitioner can create and submit
-   HoD can approve / return / reject
-   finance can validate and reserve budget
-   approved requisition is locked
-   procurement can see planning-ready work
-   non-authorized roles cannot mutate
-   strategy and budget linkage are preserved through handoff
