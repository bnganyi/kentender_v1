Demand Intake and Approval — Full PRD v1

This as a **module contract**: it should define the module boundary, lifecycle, data model, governance, roles, permissions, UX direction, integration contracts, seed expectations, and acceptance criteria clearly enough that later UI and implementation work does not have to guess.

It is anchored in:

-   concept-note direction for an end-to-end, compliance-enforced public expenditure system linking strategy, budget, procurement, contract, and outcomes, with full traceability and structured data, not just document handling
-   internal requisition requirements and implementation pack, especially around budget reservation, planning handoff, workflow stages, and role-specific work queues
-   Kenya’s procurement framework, including explicit treatment of **annual procurement plans**, **requisitions**, **e-tendering**, and related e-procurement controls in the 2020 Regulations, plus the national policy emphasis on planning, processing, contract management, inventory/asset management, compliance, and value for money ([The National Treasury](https://www.treasury.go.ke/sites/default/files/PPDA/Public-Procurement-and-Asset-Disposal-Regulations-2020.pdf))
-   OECD, UNCITRAL, and MDB/World Bank-aligned principles that public procurement begins with **needs assessment**, must be transparent and auditable, and that e-GP systems should meet structured minimum requirements for reliable public procurement processing ([OECD Legal Instruments](https://legalinstruments.oecd.org/en/instruments/OECD-LEGAL-0411?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Module purpose**

Demand Intake and Approval is the **single authoritative intake and governance layer for procurement demand**. It exists to ensure that every procurement need is:

-   formally captured
-   classified
-   linked to strategy and budget
-   approved through controlled governance
-   reserved against budget where required
-   handed off cleanly to Procurement Planning

This aligns with the concept-note direction that every shilling should remain traceable from strategy to budget to procurement to outcome, and with the internal PRD positioning requisitions as the first structured procurement demand control point.

**2\. Regulatory and best-practice anchors**

This module must be designed as **compliance by design**, not compliance by training.

Kenya’s 2020 procurement regulations explicitly include both **annual procurement planning** and **requisition** as distinct e-procurement capabilities, which supports keeping demand intake separate from planning rather than merging them into one module. Kenya’s 2020 National Public Procurement and Asset Disposal Policy also treats procurement planning, procurement processing, contract management, inventory/asset management, and compliance as connected but distinct control areas, and emphasizes fairness, transparency, competitiveness, sustainability, cost-effectiveness, and compliance enforcement. ([The National Treasury](https://www.treasury.go.ke/sites/default/files/PPDA/Public-Procurement-and-Asset-Disposal-Regulations-2020.pdf))

At the international level, OECD defines the public procurement cycle as beginning with **needs assessment** and continuing through award, contract management, and payment; UNCITRAL frames procurement around value for money, transparency, fairness, objectivity, participation, and integrity; and MDB/World Bank e-GP assessment guidance focuses on whether systems meet minimum requirements to support valid procurement processing. The module should therefore be built as a serious front-end governance layer, not just a request form. ([OECD Legal Instruments](https://legalinstruments.oecd.org/en/instruments/OECD-LEGAL-0411?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Important design rule**

This module **does not create plans**. It creates and governs **planning-ready demand**.

That matches your internal requirement that approved requisitions become plan inputs while preserving linkages, rather than planning recreating demand from scratch.

**4\. Core design decisions**

**Decision 1 — all demand becomes a demand record**

All procurement demand must resolve into a **Demand Record** in this module, even if in future it originates from a recurring plan or a system trigger.

**Decision 2 — this is not “planning”**

The module ends when demand becomes **Planning Ready**. Planning begins in the next module.

**Decision 3 — strategic linkage is mandatory, but user interaction must be tractable**

The system must preserve full strategic traceability, but the normal user path should not force every requisitioner to understand the deepest strategy hierarchy unaided.

**Decision 4 — budget is the operational control anchor**

For routine planned demand, the primary operational selector should be the **Budget Line**, from which strategy can be derived, rather than forcing target-first selection in all cases.

This is a deliberate correction to the earlier target-first bias. Your internal requisition pack proposed target-driven selection for UX simplification, but your own challenge was correct: making **Performance Target** the only primary selector is not scalable for broad operational users. The better pattern is budget-line-first with derived strategy, plus guided fallback hierarchy where needed.

**Decision 5 — exceptions are governed, not improvised**

Unplanned and emergency demand must be explicitly modeled and governed.

**Decision 6 — governance is part of the core scope**

States, transitions, approvers, lock rules, rejection loops, reservation effects, and planning trust boundary are part of the module definition, not later add-ons.

**5\. Business objectives**

The module must achieve the following:

1.  **Capture legitimate need** in a structured way
2.  **Prevent unauthorised or unbudgeted demand**
3.  **Link demand to strategy and budget**
4.  **Route demand through controlled approvals**
5.  **Create budget commitment at the correct stage**
6.  **Provide clean, role-based work queues**
7.  **Produce planning-ready demand without duplication**
8.  **Maintain a full compliance and audit chain**

**8\. Module lifecycle and governance**

**Canonical status model**

| **Status** | **Meaning** |
| --- | --- |
| Draft | Being prepared by requisitioner |
| Pending HoD Approval | Awaiting departmental business approval |
| Pending Finance Approval | Awaiting finance/budget validation |
| Approved | Fully approved and budget-reserved |
| Planning Ready | Released to Procurement Planning work queue |
| Rejected | Refused and sent back as rejected outcome |
| Cancelled | Withdrawn or terminated in a controlled way |

**Canonical transitions**

| **From** | **Action** | **To** | **Allowed role** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Draft | Submit | Pending HoD Approval | Requisitioner |
| Pending HoD Approval | Approve | Pending Finance Approval | HoD |
| Pending HoD Approval | Return | Draft | HoD |
| Pending HoD Approval | Reject | Rejected | HoD |
| Pending Finance Approval | Approve & Reserve Budget | Approved | Finance Approver |
| Pending Finance Approval | Return | Draft | Finance Approver |
| Pending Finance Approval | Reject | Rejected | Finance Approver |
| Approved | Mark Planning Ready | Planning Ready | Procurement Officer |
| Draft / Pending / Approved | Cancel | Cancelled | Authorized roles |
| Rejected | Revise & Resubmit | Pending HoD Approval | Requisitioner |
| Draft | Save | Draft | Requisitioner |

**Hard governance rules**

1.  Requisitioner must not approve their own record.
2.  HoD approval must precede finance approval in the normal path.
3.  Finance approval is the budget commitment gate.
4.  Approved and Planning Ready records are locked against ordinary edits.
5.  Return-to-draft preserves audit trail.
6.  Reject requires reason.
7.  Cancel after reservation must release reservation.
8.  No demand reaches Planning Ready without final approval.

Your test workbook and implementation pack already validate several of these, including blocking self-approval, reservation on finance approval, locking after approval, and planning-status derivation.

**9\. Budget integration model**

**Budget control principle**

No demand is finally approved without:

-   valid budget line
-   sufficient available amount
-   successful reservation

**Reservation timing**

| **Stage** | **Budget behavior** |
| --- | --- |
| Draft | No reservation |
| Pending HoD Approval | Informational checks only |
| Pending Finance Approval | Hard validation ready |
| Finance approval | Create reservation |
| Rejected / Cancelled after reservation | Release reservation |
| Approved / Planning Ready | Reservation remains active until downstream conversion/change |

**Reservation outputs**

A successful finance approval must produce:

-   reservation reference
-   reserved amount
-   available budget snapshot at validation
-   validation timestamp
-   finance approver identity

This matches your internal FR-RQ-002 and FR-RQ-006 requirements and the implementation pack’s automation on finance approval.

**Budget failure rules**

If available budget is insufficient:

-   final finance approval must be blocked
-   no reservation may be posted
-   user must receive a clear error
-   record remains at finance stage or is returned/rejected by policy

**10\. Strategy linkage model**

**Design principle**

Full strategy linkage remains mandatory, but data-entry path must be usable.

**Preferred operational path**

| **Step** | **User action** | **System behavior** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Select Budget Line | Load/derive budget context |
| 2 | Derive strategic hierarchy from Budget Line | Auto-populate strategy fields |
| 3 | Validate consistency | Block if broken mapping |

**Fallback path**

Where budget-line-first is not sufficient or data is incomplete, allow guided strategy selection:

1.  Program
2.  Sub-program
3.  Output indicator
4.  Performance target

**Policy rule**

The system must end with a full strategy mapping, but the requisitioner should not be forced into a single massive target-picker workflow.

**What gets stored**

At minimum, the record should preserve:

-   strategic plan
-   program
-   sub-program
-   output indicator
-   performance target

This preserves the concept-note requirement for end-to-end strategic traceability, while correcting the usability issue with target-only linkage.

**11\. Planning handoff model**

**Concept**

Demand Intake and Approval does **not** build procurement plans. It determines when approved demand becomes eligible for planning.

**Planning status model**

| **Planning Status** | **Meaning** |
| --- | --- |
| Not Planned | Approved but not yet taken into planning |
| Partially Planned | Part of the amount/lines linked into planning |
| Fully Planned | Entire demand linked into planning |
| Planning Ready | Operational queue state for procurement |

**Rule**

Planning consumes approved demand and creates downstream planning objects. It must not recreate demand manually.

This is supported by your internal requirement that approved requisitions become plan items and retain linkage, as well as the test cases for not planned / partially planned / fully planned states.

**12\. Functional requirements**

**FR-DIA-001 — Create demand record**

Authorized users must be able to create a demand record in Draft state.

**FR-DIA-002 — Capture structured line items**

Each demand record must support one or more line items with quantities, unit measures, unit costs, and line totals.

**FR-DIA-003 — Derive total amount**

The system must derive requested total amount from line totals.

**FR-DIA-004 — Capture justification**

The record must capture business justification and relevant specification/beneficiary context.

**FR-DIA-005 — Enforce demand classification**

The record must classify demand as Planned, Unplanned, or Emergency and apply the right governance requirements.

**FR-DIA-006 — Enforce strategy linkage**

The system must block submission if strategy linkage is missing or inconsistent.

**FR-DIA-007 — Enforce budget-line linkage**

The system must block final approval without a valid budget line.

**FR-DIA-008 — Submit for approval**

Requisitioner must be able to submit a Draft record into HoD approval flow.

**FR-DIA-009 — HoD business approval**

HoD must be able to approve, return, or reject.

**FR-DIA-010 — Finance approval and reservation**

Finance approver must be able to approve, return, or reject, with approval triggering budget reservation.

**FR-DIA-011 — Lock after approval**

Approved and Planning Ready records must be protected against normal edits.

**FR-DIA-012 — Cancel and release**

Cancellation after reservation must release reserved funds.

**FR-DIA-013 — Expose planning-ready demand**

Approved records must be visible to Procurement as planning-ready work.

**FR-DIA-014 — Preserve full audit chain**

System must log creation, submission, approval, rejection, return, cancellation, reservation, and planning-handoff changes.

**14\. Validation rules**

**14.1 Core validation**

1.  Title required
2.  Entity required
3.  Department required
4.  Requestor required
5.  Required-by date required
6.  At least one line item required
7.  Total amount must equal sum of line totals
8.  Total amount must be > 0

**14.2 Strategy validation**

1.  Full strategic linkage must resolve successfully
2.  Budget line and strategic hierarchy must be consistent
3.  If fallback hierarchy is used, system must validate all links upward

**14.3 Budget validation**

1.  Budget line must exist and be active
2.  Budget line must belong to the same entity
3.  Available budget must be sufficient at finance approval stage
4.  Reservation reference must be created on finance approval

**14.4 Demand-type validation**

**Planned**

-   standard justification required

**Unplanned**

-   justification required
-   impact\_if\_not\_procured required
-   exception flag set

**Emergency**

-   justification required
-   emergency\_justification required
-   impact\_if\_not\_procured required
-   exception flag set

**14.5 Governance validation**

1.  Requestor cannot self-approve
2.  HoD step must exist before finance step in standard flow
3.  Rejection requires reason
4.  Cancellation after reservation requires release logic
5.  Locked statuses block edits

| Save draft changes | Requisitioner, Admin | most others | non-editable states |
| Budget reservation internals | Finance, Admin, Auditor | requisitioner by default | never editable |
| Planning references | Procurement, Admin, Auditor | others by default | read-only |

**Scalability**

The work surface must use:

-   minimal top-level tabs

-   in-page filters

**Consistency**

The landing page should follow the now-proven module pattern:

-   master-detail
-   role-aware actions

**Agility**

The module must be complete enough to support real operations, but it must not absorb Planning or Tendering logic.

**24\. Explicit exclusions for v1**

To preserve sharpness, v1 excludes:

-   multi-stage exception committees
-   detailed amendment engine
-   partial approvals with auto-splitting
-   multi-department aggregated requisitions by default
-   direct supplier engagement
-   tender creation from the same screen
-   full policy-rule engine abstraction
-   public disclosure of demand records

These may come later, but are not part of the first complete version.
